SensAI – MMA Strategy Engine
SensAI is a matchup-based strategy generator designed to simulate the analytical process of an experienced fight coach. By comparing two fighters across physical attributes, technical styles, psychological traits, and rulesets, the system generates a structured fight gameplan tailored to the matchup.
The system evaluates both fighters simultaneously and produces:
Tactical breakdowns


Round-by-round strategies


Counters for opponent tendencies


Training emphasis and drills


Broadcast-style analysis segments


The quality and precision of the generated strategy depends heavily on the quality of the input data.

How SensAI Works
SensAI analyzes three primary components:
1. Fighter Profile
Your fighter’s attributes define strengths, weaknesses, and preferred strategic paths.
This includes:
Physical attributes


Striking style and level


Grappling style and level


Preferred fighting range


Signature techniques


Defensive capabilities


Tactical and psychological attributes


These inputs form the foundation of the strategic model.

2. Opponent Profile
The opponent profile allows SensAI to generate matchup-specific strategies.
Rather than producing a generic gameplan, the system compares both fighters to identify:
Advantage areas


Vulnerability windows


Tactical mismatches


Style clashes


The opponent section is critical because many strategic decisions only make sense relative to the opponent’s strengths and weaknesses.

3. Ruleset
Combat sports vary widely in scoring criteria, legal techniques, and pacing.
The ruleset determines which strategies are optimal.
For example:
Ruleset
Strategic Emphasis
UFC / MMA
Control, damage, takedowns
ONE Championship
Aggression and finishing
ADCC
Submission hunting and positional dominance
IBJJF
Guard passing and positional scoring
Boxing
Clean striking and ring control
Muay Thai
Balance, clinch work, scoring strikes

Selecting the correct ruleset allows SensAI to adjust its analysis appropriately.

Using the Application
Step 1 — Complete Your Fighter Profile
Enter your fighter’s attributes as accurately as possible.
The more detailed the profile, the more precise the resulting strategy will be.
Try to include:
realistic physical stats


your true fighting tendencies


techniques you consistently rely on


how you actually perform under pressure


Avoid rating based on theoretical skill. SensAI works best when the inputs reflect real fight behavior.

Step 2 — Enter Opponent Information
Fill in the opponent’s stats using the best available information.
Possible sources include:
fight footage


sparring experience


gym observation


scouting reports


known style archetypes


Exact values are not always required. Reasonable estimates are often sufficient for strategic analysis.

Step 3 — Select the Correct Ruleset
Choose the ruleset that matches the competition format.
Different combat sports reward different tactical priorities, and SensAI adjusts its recommendations accordingly.

Step 4 — Generate the Gameplan
Once submitted, SensAI compares both fighters and produces a detailed strategy including:
Tale of the Tape analysis


Tactical advantages and vulnerabilities


Round-by-round strategic guidance


Opponent-specific counters


Training camp emphasis



Input Guidelines
Fill Out As Many Fields As Possible
The system works best when both fighter profiles are complete.
Incomplete profiles can still generate results, but the strategy may become more generalized.

Be Realistic With Ratings
Overestimating attributes reduces the quality of the analysis.
Instead of rating based on potential, rate based on actual performance under live resistance.
Examples:
Good evaluation:
Cardio based on sparring rounds


Durability based on ability to absorb strikes


Tactical IQ based on decision making under pressure



Use Signature Moves Carefully
Select only the techniques that the fighter regularly uses in competition or live sparring.
Do not select every move the fighter has trained.
Signature techniques should represent the fighter’s most reliable offensive tools.

Estimate Opponent Stats Intelligently
If complete opponent data is not available, estimate based on observed tendencies.
Examples:
A wrestler with strong clinch control likely has high cage awareness


A fast, long-range striker may rely on movement and counters


A pressure fighter likely has higher aggression but lower patience


These estimates help SensAI model the opponent’s behavior.

Avoid Contradictory Profiles
Inputs should form a coherent fighter profile.
Examples of contradictions include:
Extremely patient fighter with maximum aggression


Long-range specialist with low footwork mobility


Submission hunter with no grappling base


Consistency in the profile leads to more accurate strategic output.

How SensAI Handles Missing Information
If some fields are left blank, SensAI may infer likely tendencies based on the rest of the profile.
For example:
A long-range striker with high footwork and karate style may be interpreted as an outside movement-based fighter.


A high aggression and power profile may be interpreted as a pressure finisher.


A wrestler with strong cage awareness may be treated as a control-based grappler.


However, inferred assumptions are less precise than explicit inputs.
Providing more data improves the quality of the analysis.

Best Practices for Accurate Gameplans
To get the best results:
Complete both fighter profiles as fully as possible


Be honest and realistic in ratings


Choose the correct ruleset


Ensure the fighter profile is internally consistent


Use reasonable estimates for opponent tendencies


The most useful strategies come from accurate scouting and realistic self-assessment.

Limitations
SensAI is designed as a strategic decision-support tool, not a prediction engine.
The generated output should be treated as:
a tactical framework


a preparation aid


a starting point for coaching discussion


Real fight outcomes depend on many factors beyond static profiles, including:
conditioning on fight night


adjustments during the bout


psychological state


real-time decision making


Use the generated gameplan as guidance, not certainty.

Tactical analysis exercises


Training camp planning
